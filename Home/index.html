Education

Masters Degree in Physics from Swansea University: Specializing in Type 2B String Theory

During my four-years at Swansea University, I engaged in two substantial research projects. In my third year, I focused on simulating quantum harmonic and anharmonic oscillators. For this, I utilized Monte Carlo methods and various statistical techniques, coding the simulations in Python.

My final-year project was in the realm of string theory. Specifically, I modeled the inter-quark potential through the AdS/CFT correspondence. I used the Mathematica language to numerically solve complex equations that couldn't be tackled analytically. Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, much of this research was self-directed and conducted from home. Through my research I was able to model the inter-quark potential at both short and far distances.
